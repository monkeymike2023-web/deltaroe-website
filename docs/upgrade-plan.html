Brand & design direction

The new logo sets the entire visual system: midnight black, antique gold, sacred geometry, the lotus, and the seven chakra lights. The site should feel like walking into a candle-lit luxury spa that happens to be run by a mystic — Aman Resorts meets a Joshua Tree apothecary. This document itself is set in that system.

The chakra spectrum rule: the seven colors appear only where they mean something — the chakra-alignment service page, the Sound of Paint series, product category markers. Everywhere else the site stays black + gold + cream. That restraint is what makes it read luxury instead of craft fair.

Typography

  • Display: a high-contrast Didone serif (Cormorant Garamond or Fraunces at light weight, self-hosted), generously letter-spaced in caps — echoing the DELTA ROE logotype with its triangle "A".
  • Body: a warm humanist serif for storytelling sections; a clean geometric sans (e.g. Jost or Outfit) for UI, prices, and booking flows.
  • Detail: gold hairline rules, small-caps eyebrows, roman-numeral section markers — the language of a fine spa menu.

Art direction

  • Dark, candle-lit photography: singing bowls with gold rim-light, crystals on black linen, incense smoke curls, hands in healing position. Shot or AI-assisted, but consistent — moody, warm, shallow depth.
  • The flower-of-life geometry from the logo as a barely-visible embossed watermark texture on dark sections (2–3% opacity).
  • Motion: one orchestrated page-load moment — the chakra column lights up the hero, top to bottom, once. Subtle gold shimmer on hover for CTAs. Nothing else moves. (prefers-reduced-motion respected.)
  • Boho warmth comes through texture and materials — linen, raw paper, dried botanicals — not through clutter.

IV

The booking engine — the #1 revenue lever

Nothing else in this plan pays off faster than making booking effortless and rebooking automatic.

Always one tap away

Persistent gold "Book a Session" button in the header and as a mobile floating bar. Every service page books directly in-page — no detour to a generic calendar.

First-timer funnel

A "New to energy healing?" path: free Discovery Call or a First Visit ritual bundle (Reiki + Sound Bath intro, $129) that removes the fear of the unknown.

Packages & series

3-session and 6-session packs at 10–15% off, paid up front. Prepaid packages raise retention and smooth cash flow — the spa-industry standard.

Deposits & reminders

Card-on-file deposits kill no-shows; automated SMS/email reminders with one-tap reschedule protect every hour on the table.

The rebooking loop

Post-session email: session notes / aftercare + "Book your next alignment" with the calendar pre-filtered. 48-hour rebooking discount window.

Upsell at checkout

Booking confirmation offers the matching product: booked a chakra alignment → chakra candle set; sound bath → the recorded 432 Hz library.

New revenue-bearing offerings to add

  • Group sound baths (6–10 people × $35–45) — same hour, multiplied revenue; the community on-ramp to 1:1 work.
  • Couples / duo sessions at a premium.
  • Corporate wellness page — on-site sound baths & guided meditation for Sacramento-area offices; one contract can be worth a month of singles.
  • Gift cards — promoted hard at Mother's Day, Valentine's, holidays. Pure prepaid margin.
  • Reiki certification workshops (Level I/II) — Tamika is a Reiki Master; teaching is the highest-leverage weekend format ($225–$425/student × 6–10 students).

SEO — owning Elk Grove & south Sacramento

Local SEO (the fastest wins)

  • Google Business Profile overhaul: all services listed with prices, booking link, weekly photo uploads, Q&A seeded, posts for every event. GBP is the #1 local ranking factor and it's free.
  • Citations: consistent name/address/phone on Yelp, Apple Maps, Bing Places, Nextdoor, MindBody directories, wellness aggregators.
  • "Best in Elk Grove" positioning: the review record backs it — say it, with the receipts, on the homepage and the /reviews page.
  • Local landmarks content: "in historic Old Town Elk Grove" woven into copy — matches how locals search and how AI describes places.

VIII

AI-search optimization (GEO)

A growing share of "reiki near me, is it worth it?" questions are now asked to ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini. The sites those engines cite share traits Delta Roe can adopt wholesale:

  • Direct-answer content structure: every service page opens with a plain 2–3 sentence answer ("A reiki session at Delta Roe in Elk Grove lasts 30 minutes and costs $144…") before the storytelling. AI engines quote the quotable.
  • FAQ pages with real questions, marked up with FAQPage schema — the single highest-yield AI-visibility asset for a local business.
  • llms.txt at the site root summarizing the business, services, prices, location, and booking URL for AI crawlers.
  • Entity consistency: identical business facts across site, GBP, Yelp, Facebook — AI engines cross-check sources before recommending.
  • Third-party mentions: local press (Elk Grove Citizen), Sacramento wellness roundups, podcast guest spots — citations are what make an AI engine confident enough to name a business.
  • Own the branded answers: pages that define "Sound-Integrated Self-Defense™" and "The Sound of Paint" so any AI asked about them has exactly one authoritative source.
IX

Reviews & social proof engine

  • Ask at the peak: automated review request 2 hours post-session (the afterglow window), direct Google-review deep link. QR card handed at checkout.
  • Surface everywhere: live Google rating badge in the site header, rotating review quotes on every service page matched to that service, full /reviews page with schema.
  • Video testimonials: 3–5 short clips of clients describing their first session — the single most persuasive asset for a skeptical first-timer, reusable on social and ads.
  • Respond to every review — response quality is itself a local-ranking and AI-trust signal.
