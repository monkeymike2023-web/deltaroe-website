2 · Day-to-Day: Bookings (Square Appointments)

  • Your calendar: the Square Appointments app (phone or computer) shows every booking; clients book from the website's "Book a Session" buttons.
  • Change a price or session length: Square dashboard → Items & Services → edit. Live immediately. (Tell your web person too, so the website's displayed menu matches — see section 5.)
  • Deposits / no-show protection: Square settings let you require a card or deposit at booking.
  • Gift cards & memberships: sold and tracked in Square; the website's Gift Cards and Memberships pages point clients there.
  • Getting paid: Square deposits to your bank account (standard next-business-day). Fees only when a card is charged (~2.6% + 10¢).

3 · Day-to-Day: The Apothecary (Shopify, when live)

  • Orders: appear in your Shopify dashboard; your suppliers (candles, oils, crystals, apparel) make and ship each order under the Delta Roe label — you never pack a box.
  • Add / edit / price a product: Shopify → Products. Photos, description, price — yours to control.
  • Switching suppliers: you are locked into no one. A local artisan's product can join or replace any item — new photos + description + their fulfillment arrangement.
  • Money: Shopify pays out to your bank; margins on the private-label plan run ~45–65% depending on product.
  • Until the store opens: the shop page on the site is a hidden preview (search engines are told to ignore it). Nothing can be bought there.

7 · Launch Checklist

  1. Your sign-off — prices, product names, membership tiers, Roe's voice, the chakra page
  2. Square Appointments setup — your services, hours, deposit rules (a few days; we assist)
  3. Booking buttons switch from the Wix scheduler to Square
  4. deltaroe.com points to the new site — the switch takes a day; Wix keeps working until that moment
  5. Google updates — Business Profile points to the new site; analytics begin counting
  6. Celebrate. Then (on your schedule): shop samples → your approval → store opens (4–6 weeks, independent of the website launch)

8 · Ownership — What Is Yours

  • Everything. The design, all code, all writing, the chakra experience, Roe and her knowledge base, images, and the brand work — yours.
  • All customer data — bookings (your Square account), orders (your Shopify account), from day one. The website itself stores no customer data.
  • At handover: the code repository and hosting move into accounts you own; your web person stays on as an invited collaborator, not the owner. The domain is already yours.
  • Independence: the site is standard, well-documented technology (Next.js) — any competent web developer could maintain it, not just the person who built it.
